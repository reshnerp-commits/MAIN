Core Features

Evidence-Based Digital Safety

Our solutions are built on peer-reviewed research and industry best practices from leading institutions

Privacy Protection

Learn advanced techniques to protect your personal data online. According to Pew Research Center (2023), 81% of Americans feel they have little to no control over their data collection.

Source: Pew Research Center, "Americans and Privacy," 2023

Critical Thinking

Develop skills to identify misinformation. Stanford University research shows that 82% of middle schoolers couldn't distinguish between sponsored content and real news.

Source: Stanford History Education Group, 2022

Digital Wellness

Maintain healthy screen time habits. The World Health Organization reports that excessive screen time is linked to increased anxiety and depression in adolescents.

Source: WHO Guidelines on Physical Activity, 2023

Cybersecurity Basics

Master password security and 2FA. Verizon's 2023 DBIR reports that 86% of data breaches involve compromised credentials, making strong authentication critical.

Source: Verizon Data Breach Investigations Report, 2023

Anti-Cyberbullying

Create safer online communities. CDC data shows that 16% of high school students experienced cyberbullying, with significant mental health impacts.

Source: CDC Youth Risk Behavior Survey, 2023

Digital Footprint

Manage your online reputation. Research shows that 70% of employers have rejected candidates based on their social media presence.

Source: CareerBuilder Survey, 2023
